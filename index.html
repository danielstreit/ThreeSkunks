-A Monty Hall Predicament

The gamemakers have trapped you in a hallway with three mysterious doors. You hear a voice over the loud speaker:

Behind each door is a skunk. One of these skunks has been descented. Choose your exit wisely.
-the gamemakers

Are you sure you want door ? The gamemakers offer more information:

Out of the goodness of our hearts, we will help you avoid a stinky situation. From the two doors you have not selected, we can assure you that door definitely has a stinky skunk behind it. Door may also have a stinky skunk...or it may have the descented skunk. Would you like to keep door or switch to door ?
-the gamemakers

Initially, the probability of the descented skunk being behind any of the doors is equal. You'll pick the correct door 1 out of 3 tries and the wrong door 2 out of 3 tries.

When the gamemakers remove a door from the unchosen doors, these probabilities are not effected. The initial choice will still be correct 1 out of 3 times and wrong 2 out of 3 times. But now, because only one door remains of the unchosen, the probability that it is correct is 2/3, the same probability that the initial pick was wrong. So, the best strategy is to pick any door initially and always switch when given the option.
